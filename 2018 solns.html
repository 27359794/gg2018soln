Let GGG be group of orientation preserving symmetries of octahedron. The octahedron has 4 pairs of opposite faces. Let XXX be the set of these pairs XXX. We prove that X≅S4X \cong S_4X≅S4​.

First, the action of GGG on XXX somehow permutes the elements of XXX, and S4S_4S4​ is the group of 4-element permutations, so there exists a homomorphism from GGG to S4S_4S4​. We also know that ∣S4∣=4!=24|S_4| = 4! = 24∣S4​∣=4!=24 and that ∣G∣=24|G| = 24∣G∣=24 (the latter by an orbit-stabiliser argument — any particular vertex has an orbit of size 6, and if we fix that vertex there are 4 stabilisers. 6 x 4 = 24), so if we establish that the homomorphism is surjective then it is also bijective and we are done.

Establishing surjectivity means proving that we can generate any permutation of XXX by the action of GGG. Since S4S_4S4​ is generated by transpositions of elements (i.e. two elements swap and the rest stay where they are), it suffices to show that GGG can swap any pair of elements of XXX and leave the rest where they are.

Let A,B,C,DA, B, C, DA,B,C,D be some labelling of the pairs of faces; we can exchange A,BA, BA,B while keeping C,DC, DC,D where they are by rotating the octahedron 180o through the axis orthogonal to the line through the centre of the opposite faces in AAA, and also orthogonal to the line through the centre of the opposite faces in BBB. (This may be hard to visualise but I think it works). This completes the proof.
